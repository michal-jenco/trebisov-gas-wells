Introduction

What Are These Structures?

The cross-shaped metal assemblies rising from concrete pads across this field are called Christmas Trees — vianočné stromčeky in Slovak, or more formally ústia vrtov (wellheads). Each one sits directly on top of a single gas well drilled between 2,250 and 2,607 metres straight down into the earth. TERAZ.sk

The field produced natural gas from 1996 until 2015, when the deposit was declared depleted and all extraction ceased. Since then the equipment has been left in place — still bolted to the underground casing system — as part of the regulated abandonment process required by Slovak mining regulations.

Two distinct generations of wellhead are present on the same field. The blue Christmas trees are older Soviet-era designs using gate valves with boxy square actuator housings. The yellow Christmas trees represent a newer 1996-era generation using ball valves — the spherical bodies visible along the vertical stack — with pneumatic actuators allowing remote or automatic operation. Slovak Wikipedia

Both generations were installed and operated by NAFTA VÝCHOD a.s., the eastern Slovak subsidiary created during NAFTA's controversial 1996 privatisation and headquartered in Michalovce. All five wells tap into the same geology below: a Neogene sandstone reservoir packed with high-purity methane (94–99% CH₄) at depth. MDPI — Processes 2021

Component Reference

Components Explained

Every major part of both wellhead designs explained in English and Slovak.

🔵 Blue Tree — Soviet-era Gate Valve Design

Valve type: Gate valves — a metal wedge slides across the bore. Many turns to open/close. Extremely robust but slow.

Actuators: Manual handwheels only — operator must be physically present.

Body shape: Large, boxy, square housings — classic Soviet industrial aesthetic. Built for field repairability.

Era: 1960s–1980s Czechoslovak oilfield equipment. nafta.sk history

🟡 Yellow Tree — Ball Valve Design (1996)

Valve type: Ball valves — a sphere rotates 90° to open/close. Quarter-turn = much faster emergency shutoff.

Actuators: Pneumatic actuator disc on left wing arm — compressed air operates valve remotely or automatically.

Body shape: Rounder, more compact — the spherical ball bodies give the tree its "bubble" silhouette.

Era: Mid-1990s, matching the 28 October 1996 nameplate date. TERAZ.sk

1
Pressure Gauge
Tlakomer / Manometer
🇬🇧 English

Mechanical dial at the very top reading static wellbore pressure at all times. On an active well it shows reservoir pressure minus friction losses. On an abandoned well it must read zero — any non-zero reading signals gas is still migrating upward through the casing, a serious safety issue requiring investigation.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Mechanický ciferník na vrchu, zobrazujúci statický tlak vrtu. Na aktívnom vrte ukazuje tlak zásobníka. Na opustenom vrte musí ukazovať nulu — akákoľvek nenulová hodnota signalizuje migráciu plynu nahor a vyžaduje vyšetrenie.

2
Tree Cap
Uzáver stromu / Kryt ústia vrtu
🇬🇧 English

Flanged cap bolted to the topmost connection, sealing the wellbore from the atmosphere. On a working well it is removed when wireline tools enter from above. On these decommissioned wells it is almost certainly permanently sealed — possibly even welded — as required by Slovak mining abandonment regulations.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Prírubový uzáver tesniaci vrt od atmosféry. Na funkčnom vrte sa odšraubuje pre vstup drôtových nástrojov. Na odstavených vrtoch je takmer určite natrvalo utesnený podľa slovenskej banskej legislatívy.

3
Upper Master Valve
Horný hlavný ventil / Horný uzáverový ventil
🇬🇧 English

The primary emergency shutoff. Gate valve form (blue tree): metal wedge slides across the bore — slow but robust. Ball valve form (yellow tree): sphere rotates 90° — quarter-turn, far faster emergency response. Both create a metal-to-metal seat rated for the full 35 MPa stamped on the nameplate. The yellow tree's pneumatic actuator suggests partial remote operation was designed in.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Primárny núdzový uzáver. Šúpadlová forma (modrý strom): klin kĺže cez otvor — pomalé, ale odolné. Guľová forma (žltý strom): guľa sa otočí o 90° — štvrťotočka, oveľa rýchlejšia reakcia. Oba vytvárajú kovové sedlo na plný tlak 35 MPa podľa štítku.

4
Wing Valves — Left & Right
Krídlové ventily — ľavý a pravý
🇬🇧 English

The horizontal arms of the Christmas tree cross. The right wing is the main production outlet — gas flowed out here to the Trebišov Gas Collection Station TERAZ.sk. The left wing connects to the casing annulus (the space between underground pipe strings) for monitoring and bleeding any migrated gas. The large pneumatic disc on the yellow tree's left arm enables remote operation via compressed air signal.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Vodorovné ramená vianočného stromčeka. Pravé krídlo je hlavným vývodom — plyn tu vytekával do Zberného plynového strediska Trebišov. Ľavé krídlo sa pripája k medzikružiu plášťa pre monitorovanie. Pneumatický pohon na ľavom krídle žltého stromu umožňuje diaľkové ovládanie.

5
Production Cross (PK)
Produkčný kríž / Ťažobný kríž
🇬🇧 English

The specific component described on the nameplate — PK, 2 1/16″ × 2 1/16″, 35 MPa. Machined from a single forged steel billet, it is the structural hub where vertical wellbore flow meets the horizontal arms. The cross design enables access from all four directions: above for tool entry, below from the well, and left and right for production and annulus control.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Konkrétna súčiastka popísaná na typovom štítku — PK, 2 1/16″ × 2 1/16″, 35 MPa. Opracovaná z jedného bloku kovanej ocele, je štrukturálnym centrom. Krížový dizajn umožňuje prístup zo všetkých štyroch smerov.

6
Swab Valve
Čistiaci / Servisný ventil
🇬🇧 English

Positioned between the two master valves, this provides controlled access for running tools into the well — wireline logging instruments, pressure sensors, or rubber-tipped swabbing tools (from which the name comes) historically used to lift accumulated liquids. With both master valves sealing below, opening this valve allows safe tool access during maintenance or inspection.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Umiestnený medzi dvoma hlavnými ventilmi, poskytuje prístup pre nástroje — karotážne prístroje, tlakové senzory alebo gumové čistiace nástroje. S oboma hlavnými ventilmi zatvoreným nižšie umožňuje bezpečný servisný prístup.

7
Lower Master Valve
Dolný hlavný ventil
🇬🇧 English

A second full-bore shutoff providing mandatory redundancy. Regulations require two independent master valves so the well remains sealed even if one fails or needs maintenance. Closing this valve is the strongest possible safety position — it fully isolates the production tubing from everything above.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Druhý uzáver s plným prietokom — povinný záložný stupeň. Predpisy vyžadujú dva nezávislé hlavné ventily. Zatvorenie tohto ventilu úplne izoluje rúrkovú kolonu od všetkého nad ňou.

8
Tubing Head Spool
Hlava produkčnej rúrky
🇬🇧 English

A large flanged spool that physically suspends and seals the production tubing string — the narrow inner pipe (typically 50–75 mm diameter) running the full 2,250–2,600 m to the reservoir TERAZ.sk. Inside sits a tubing hanger — a machined conical seat from which the tubing string hangs under its own considerable weight. Side ports allow independent pressure monitoring of the annular space around the tubing.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Veľká prírubová tvarovka nesúca a tesniaca produkčnú rúrkovú kolonu — úzku rúru (50–75 mm) vedúcu celých 2 250–2 600 m k zásobníku. Vo vnútri je závesník rúrky, kužeľové sedlo. Bočné otvory umožňujú monitorovanie tlaku medzikružia.

9
Casing Head
Hlava plášťa / Plášťová hlava
🇬🇧 English

The first permanent fixture at surface level — welded onto the outermost casing string, providing the first pressure containment. The entire Christmas tree above bolts to its top flange. Think of it as the foundation for everything visible: all valves, spools, and fittings ultimately stand on this one component, which is itself anchored to concentric steel casing pipes cemented deep into the earth.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Prvá trvalá armatúra na povrchu — privarená na vonkajší plášť, poskytujúca prvé tlakové zadržanie. Celý vianočný stromček je priskrutkovaný k jej hornej prírube. Je základom všetkého viditeľného nad zemou.

10
Casing Outlet Assemblies & Annulus Gauges
Vývody plášťa a manometre medzikružia
🇬🇧 English

Horizontal branch assemblies on the grating platform, each connecting to a different annular space between concentric casing strings underground. The blue tree's two separate assemblies indicate at least three concentric casing strings — like Russian dolls of steel pipe, each cemented at a different depth. Any gauge reading above zero signals potential casing integrity failure, even on a decommissioned well. MDPI — Processes 2021

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Horizontálne vývodové zostavy na plošine, každá sa pripájajúca k inému medzikružiu medzi sústredne uloženými rúrami plášťa. Dve zostavy modrého stromu naznačujú aspoň tri sústredné plášte — ako matriosky zo ocele. Tlak nad nulou signalizuje potenciálne zlyhanie integrity plášťa.

11
Conductor Flange & Wellhead Cellar
Príruba vodiaceho plášťa a vrtoví pivnica
🇬🇧 English

The large round disc at the very base is the top flange of the conductor pipe — the outermost, widest casing, driven in first and cemented to approximately 30–100 m depth. Everything above bolts to this. Below it, under the steel grating, is the wellhead cellar — a shallow excavated pit giving access to cables and instrument lines, and acting as a sump to safely collect any leaking fluids.

🇸🇰 Slovenčina

Veľký okrúhly disk pri základni je horná príruby vodiaceho potrubia — najvonkajšieho, najširšieho plášťa, zacementovaného do ~30–100 m hĺbky. Pod ním, pod roštom, je vrtoví pivnica — jama pre prístup k káblom, slúžiaca tiež ako zberná nádrž.

Historical Context

NAFTA & Trebišov — A History

From Europe's earliest oil discovery to Slovakia's first geothermal well conversion — over a century of industrial history connected to this field.

1913–1914
Oil discovered at Gbely in western Slovakia (then Austro-Hungarian Empire) — one of the first commercial oilfields in continental Europe. Industrial production began 13 January 1914. The "NAFTA-UD GBELY" name stamped on the wellhead equipment you found traces directly back to this moment. nafta.sk Slovak Spectator — 100 Years
1958–1965
Slovak gas production peaks at 1.1–1.3 billion m³ per year in the early 1960s, almost entirely from the Vienna Basin in western Slovakia. By mid-decade, those fields are declining. State geologists begin seismic surveys further east into the East Slovak Basin (Východoslovenská panva) — a Miocene-age tectonic depression stretching from Michalovce to the Ukrainian border, geologically distinct from the Vienna Basin and largely unexplored for hydrocarbons. SME — 90 Years of Slovak Oil
1966
NAFTA begins natural gas production in eastern Slovakia. The East Slovak Lowland and its Badenian–Sarmatian sandstone reservoirs are confirmed as commercially productive. The Trebišov area — sitting in the Trebišov tectonic depression, the easternmost part of the East Slovak Basin — is identified as a priority exploration target. The state enterprise operating the wells is known as Slovenské naftové závody (SNZ), later renamed NAFTA in 1974. nafta.sk MDPI Geosciences — Trebišov Depression Geology
1970–1980
The Brotherhood pipeline (Bratstvo) — the massive Soviet gas export corridor across Slovakia — enters operation in 1972, transforming Slovakia into a major gas transit country. Domestic production in the East Slovak Lowland expands to supply the national grid. NAFTA drills wells in the Trebišov dobývací priestor (mining zone) during this decade; the blue Christmas tree wellheads in your photographs, with their characteristic Soviet-era boxy gate valve actuators, date architecturally from this period of heavy Soviet-influenced equipment supply. Pravda — 50 Years of the Brotherhood Pipeline
1974–1990
The state enterprise is renamed NAFTA, národný podnik Gbely in 1974, then enters the centralised Naftový a plynárenský priemysel conglomerate in 1977. By the late 1980s, NAFTA employs over 2,000 workers and manages wells across both the Vienna Basin and East Slovak Lowland. Gas production in eastern Slovakia falls gradually from its 1960s peak — by 1987, total national gas output had declined to under 500 million m³/year — but the Trebišov field remains in active production, supplying the regional grid via gathering lines and the nearby compressor station. After the 1989 Velvet Revolution the state conglomerate is broken up: SPP (Slovenský plynárenský priemysel) takes over gas sales and import; NAFTA retains exploration and production. Slovak Wikipedia — NAFTA History
1993
Dissolution of Czechoslovakia; NAFTA becomes a Slovak state company. The national field network includes 270 registered wells across the East Slovak Lowland. MDPI — Processes 2021
August 1996
NAFTA is controversially privatised. The Fund of National Property sells the government's 45.9% stake for approximately 500 million Slovak crowns (~$16.6 million) — roughly one-seventh of market value — to Druhá obchodná a.s., a company linked to HZDS official Vladimír Poór. The opposition calls it the "theft of the century" (lúpež storočia). The company is restructured into subsidiaries, including NAFTA VÝCHOD a.s. in Michalovce, which takes over management of the Trebišov wells. RFE/RL Slovak Spectator Slovak Wikipedia
28 October 1996
The nameplate you found is stamped and certified on this exact date. Serial V-900405 is the 70th component quality-certified by NAFTA that year, installed at one of the Trebišov 8–12 wells during active field operations under the newly created NAFTA VÝCHOD. TERAZ.sk
1998–1999
Following the 1998 elections, criminal charges are filed against Poór and 40.9% of shares are returned to state ownership. International investors subsequently enter the ownership structure. Slovak Wikipedia
2015
NAFTA terminates gas extraction at the Trebišov deposit. Wells Trebišov 8–12 are officially decommissioned. The Christmas tree assemblies in your photographs are left in place as required by regulated safe abandonment procedures. TERAZ.sk SLOVGAS
2019–2020
NAFTA and partner Aspect Holdings acquire the Beša Exploration Area (771 km²) and Pavlovce nad Uhom Exploration Area (1,070 km²) — targeting new potential gas reserves in the East Slovak Lowland. nafta.sk
2021
An academic study published in MDPI Processes maps the state of all 270 NAFTA-registered wells across Slovakia, including the East Slovak Lowland. It finds that as of 2020, 198 of 270 wells are plugged and abandoned, 61 are drilled and equipped (including the Trebišov field), and only six active extraction deposits remain in the eastern lowland. The study notes a critical lack of uniform electronic monitoring records — exactly the documentation gap that this photographic project aims to address. MDPI — Processes: Slovak Gas Well Mapping
February 2024
The Slovak Ministry of Environment approves NAFTA's geothermal conversion plan (EIA opinion 6 February 2024). Wells Trebišov 8–12 will be repurposed as closed-loop geothermal heat exchangers producing approximately 0.9–1 MW of thermal energy for Trebišov's district heating network — the first such project in Slovakia. TERAZ.sk — MŽP Approval SLOVGAS

Geology

Why There Is Gas Under Trebišov

The East Slovak Basin is a textbook example of a Neogene back-arc basin — and understanding its geology explains both the gas that was extracted and the geothermal heat that will be harvested next.

The East Slovak Basin is a Neogene back-arc basin — it formed when tectonic plates shifted over the last 20 million years, creating a rift-like depression that slowly filled with sediment from surrounding highlands. USGS — Pannonian Basin The Trebišov Depression, the specific sub-basin beneath the town, contains sedimentary sequences up to 2,000 metres thick, built up from calcareous clays, sandstone layers, and volcanic ash from the Slanské vrchy mountains visible to the west.

The gas reservoirs sit in Badenian and Lower Sarmatian sandstone layers — porous rock about 15 million years old, buried deep enough for organic matter to transform into methane over geological time. The gas is 94–99% methane (CH₄), extremely high purity. MDPI — Processes 2021

The same high geothermal gradient that makes these wells valuable for geothermal conversion today is also part of what originally drove the hydrocarbon maturation. The East Slovak Basin has a heat flow of 90–125 mW/m² — well above the European average — and subsurface temperatures reach approximately 100°C at 1,700 m depth. MDPI — Resources 2021 MDPI — Applied Sciences 2022

Future Use

The Geothermal Conversion

♻ Slovakia's First Geothermal Well Conversion — Approved February 2024

The wells you photographed are not simply abandoned. NAFTA has secured government approval to convert Trebišov 8–12 into closed-loop geothermal heat exchangers, feeding the city's district heating network. TERAZ.sk SLOVGAS

Rather than pumping gas up, the converted wells will circulate heat-exchange fluid down to 1,700 m depth and back up, extracting warmth from the naturally hot rock. At approximately 0.9–1 MW of thermal output, this is the first project of its kind in Slovakia. The high geothermal gradient of the East Slovak Basin — 90–125 mW/m² — makes these ageing boreholes surprisingly well-suited for a second life as heating infrastructure. MDPI — Resources 2021

The equipment in your photographs — all that rust and peeling yellow paint — stands at the crossroads of two eras: the fossil fuel past of Soviet-era Czechoslovak industry, and a clean energy future being built on the same boreholes drilled more than thirty years ago.

🔵 LEFT — During Active Production (1996–2015)

During production, gas flowed upward through the narrow production tubing (the innermost cyan pipe, ~50 mm diameter) running the full 2,250–2,607 m from the Badenian sandstone reservoir. A packer — an inflatable rubber seal — sat at ~1,700 m to isolate the annular space between the tubing and the casing, preventing gas from bypassing the tubing. The multiple concentric casing strings (conductor, surface, intermediate, and long-string production casing), each cemented at different depths, provided structural integrity and kept the borehole from collapsing. At the top, the Christmas tree valves controlled flow to the Gas Collection Station.

NPC — Well Plugging & Abandonment (2011)
🟢 RIGHT — After Plug & Abandonment (post-2015)

After decommissioning, the well must be permanently sealed against any future hydrocarbon migration. Engineers set multiple independent cement barrier plugs at critical points: a formation plug at reservoir depth to seal the source rock; a mechanical bridge plug just above it as a platform for cement; a mid-well plug isolating different pressure zones; and an aquifer protection plug preventing any hydrocarbons from contaminating shallow groundwater. The entire tubing interior is filled with cement. A final surface plug seals the top. The Christmas trees you photographed remain bolted in place above all of this — sealed, but physically still there. The Slovak mining authority requires wells to remain in this state under monitoring.

🔗 How This Connects Directly to the Trebišov Wells

The nameplate you found — stamped 35 MPa / BORE 50.8 mm / 28.10.1996 — is the engineering specification for the production cross sitting at the very top of the left-hand diagram above. The 35 MPa rating matches the pressure class required for the reservoir depth of 2,250–2,607 m. The 50.8 mm bore is the inner diameter of the tubing shown running down the centre of the diagram.

The right-hand diagram is the current physical state of all five Trebišov wells as of 2015. The Christmas trees you photographed are the surface expression of this permanently sealed underground structure. The geothermal conversion approved in 2024 will involve returning to a configuration between these two states: re-opening access at ~1,700 m depth, circulating heat-exchange fluid, but never re-perforating the reservoir — keeping the formation plug permanently in place.
